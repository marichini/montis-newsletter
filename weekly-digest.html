UBS demos permissionless blockchain compliance at infrastructure level

UBS and Nethermind have completed two proofs of concept on the Ethereum Sepolia test network, demonstrating that banks can embed compliance controls directly into Ethereum's block production pipeline. This development has significant implications for the use of DLT in capital markets, particularly in terms of regulatory compliance. From a Montis perspective, this innovation could potentially streamline our own compliance processes and enhance the overall efficiency of our DLT-based services.

↗ SOURCE · June 23, 2026

Tokenisation is an opportunity – if the EU is proactive

The EU's capital market is fragmented, but tokenisation could be a solution. According to Lidia Kurt from Seturion, a subsidiary of Boerse Stuttgart, the EU needs to be proactive in creating a regulatory framework that supports tokenisation. Montis, as a CSD, is well-positioned to play a key role in the development of tokenised assets and could benefit from a more favourable regulatory environment.

↗ SOURCE · June 23, 2026
📜

EU regulation and tokenisation

The EU's regulatory framework for tokenisation is still in development, but it has the potential to shape the future of capital markets. A proactive approach to regulation could create new opportunities for market participants, including Montis, and help to establish the EU as a leader in the use of DLT for capital markets. However, a fragmented or overly restrictive regulatory environment could hinder innovation and growth.

↗ SOURCE · June 23, 2026

Global economic trends and their impact on capital markets

The current global economic trends, including inflation and interest rates, have significant implications for capital markets. As a CSD, Montis needs to be aware of these trends and their potential impact on our business and the broader economy. The use of DLT and tokenisation could potentially help to mitigate some of the risks associated with these trends, but it is essential to stay informed and adapt to changing market conditions.
