Big News of the Week
The 4 stories that mattered
🚀 Markets · SpaceX IPO

SpaceX SPCX debuts Nasdaq at $161.11 (+19%) — history's largest IPO raised $75B, $2.1T market cap (Jun 12)

SpaceX priced at $135/share and closed its first Nasdaq day at $161.11 (+19%), touching an intraday high of $176.52 (+31%). The IPO raised $75 billion — the largest in history, dwarfing Saudi Aramco's 2019 record. SpaceX's market cap hit ~$2.1 trillion on debut. The listing lifted S&P 500 +0.50% on Friday, helping markets end the week positive (+0.65% to 7,431) despite a turbulent CPI and ECB week. Elon Musk became the world's first reported trillionaire on paper.

↗ CNBC · Jun 12
📈 Central Banks · ECB

ECB raises rates for first time in 3 years — +25bp to 2.25%, Lagarde keeps door open to further hikes (Jun 11)

The ECB Governing Council hiked the deposit facility rate from 2.00% to 2.25% on June 11 — the first rate hike since 2023, driven by stagflationary pressures from the Iran war and its oil-price impact. ECB President Lagarde stated it was "not an insurance hike" and explicitly left the door open to further tightening. ING analysts now price a >60% chance of a second hike in September. European bond markets repriced; OAT and BTP spreads widened. EUR/USD initially rallied on the hawkish surprise before retracing.

↗ Euronews / ING Think · Jun 11
🤖 AI Governance · Anthropic

Trump bans all federal agencies from using Anthropic — federal judge blocks order, rules it illegal retaliation (Jun 9-12)

President Trump ordered all federal agencies and military contractors to cease business with Anthropic, after the company refused to remove guardrails preventing Claude's use in autonomous weapons and mass domestic surveillance. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth labelled Anthropic a "supply chain risk." Within days, a federal judge issued a temporary injunction — ruling the Pentagon's declaration was retaliation for Anthropic's First Amendment rights. OpenAI reportedly struck a deal with DoD under the same terms Anthropic refused. Major AI governance signal for the regulated financial sector.

↗ CBS News / Defense One · Jun 12
🕊️ Geopolitics · Iran Peace Deal

US-Iran agree final text of peace deal — signing ceremony set for June 19 in Switzerland, Strait of Hormuz to reopen (Jun 11-14)

Following intense diplomacy (Pakistan PM Sharif mediating), the US and Iran agreed on the "final, agreed text" of a peace deal ending the 2026 Iran war and reopening the Strait of Hormuz. The formal signing is set for Friday June 19 in Switzerland. Brent crude fell sharply from ~$93 on the news. Markets rallied Friday — S&P +0.50%. Key risk this week: the deal is not yet signed, Trump's Thursday warning "get their act together" highlighted fragility. If signing goes ahead, Brent could fall to the mid-$70s on restored Iranian supply.

↗ NBC News / CNBC · Jun 14

"Chain Reaction: What the Pentagon-Anthropic Dispute Means for Civilian Agencies Across All Levels of Government"

This week's most important read for anyone working at the intersection of AI and regulated institutions. CDT unpacks the downstream implications of Trump's Anthropic ban — not just for federal agencies, but for any institution (including financial market infrastructure) that deploys AI under regulatory oversight. Direct read-across to how Montis and its partners should be thinking about AI governance clauses in their own infrastructure agreements.
